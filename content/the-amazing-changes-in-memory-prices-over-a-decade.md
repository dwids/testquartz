…which shows the 1,000 times increase in microSD card capacity in 9 years, set me off and running about my own storage costs over that time.

| Year | Type | MB | Cost | $/MB | Comment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 2004 | CF | 100 | $200 | $2.00 | For my Nikon D70 camera. They proudly said it was the first DSLR under $2000. Which, at $1999 was true. Except you couldn't actually **use** it as they didn't give you a CF card. |
| 2014 | SD (HC) | 32,000 | $28 | $0.00088 | 32 GB Sandisk Ultra Class 10 (MSY 11 Dec 2014). That's 0.088 of 1 cent. |
| 1994 | HDD | 340 | $475 | $1.40 | 340 MB disk drive. Cheaper per MB than the CF card 10 years later. |



Hard Disk Drives are/were cheaper per MB than these little cards. If I look back another 10 years to 1994, my 340 MB  (!) disk drive [2]  cost me \$475.   So that’s \$1.40 per MB. Cheaper than the CF card which was 10 years later.
