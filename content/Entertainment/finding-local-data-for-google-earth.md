I&#8217;m using [Google Earth](http://earth.google.com/) a fair bit. I&#8217;ve been fascinated with turning maps into 3D views since secondary school; when we learned how to create a height versus distance cross-section from a topology map.

Google Earth has the ability to cut and paste latitude & longitude co-ords directly in, which makes it quite easy to Go To a place of interest.

A number of web sites have lat-long listings &#8211; such as this [Australian Government](http://www.ga.gov.au/geodesy/astro/sunrise.jsp#gaz) one. This &#8216;only&#8217; goes to the **minute **resolution, eg *S 37° 52&#8242; E 144° 54&#8242;*  This is okay for big things like lakes or towns, but not fine enough for finding smaller locations. It&#8217;s just too large an area on the (Google) Earth.

Thank goodness for the newish hobby of Geocaching. Some of their sites have [searchable  ](http://geocaching.com.au/search/)lat-long listings down to the **fraction of second.** For example, this is for a point on a bridge near Walhalla in Victoria:   S *37° 51.309 E 146° 25.993  *

Noite: if using this format in Google Earth, you have to make a simple change or two; **&#8211; 37° 51.309, 146° 25.993**  The &#8216;-&#8216; means South.

I&#8217;ve found a [few ](http://www.swopnet.com/waypoints/gps/australia3.html)other [sites ](http://soaring.aerobatics.ws/TP/Bacchus/bacchus.hnd.txt)that also have Victorian/Australian listings.
