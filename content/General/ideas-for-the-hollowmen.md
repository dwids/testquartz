I&#8217;ve already expressed surprise &#8211; and a tinge of sadness &#8211; that The Hollowmen is [just not cutting through](__GHOST_URL__/2008/08/01/hollowmen-barely-making-top-50-in-ratings/) in the ratings. In thinking about it some more, a few things come to mind as to possible reasons.

##### Similar Story Arc (plot) in each show?

Government in trouble over issue.  Tony has to solve it. Phillip and Warren provide the official guidelines/suggestions (usually involving a PowerPoint and a preliminary report in 6 months). Tony has to work around them without offending them. Sometimes a focus group is run.  Murph thinks of clever solution. End.  (I know I&#8217;m exaggerating, but you get the point)

##### Too Narrow Cast of Characters?

*Frontline* had a number of more independent  lead roles, so multiple threads could be developed. HollowMen is a bit more linear. Bit hard to explain what I mean, perhaps the Suggestions bit at the end will cover it.

##### Little Buy-In From Viewers?

Do we just assume this sort of thing goes on anyway.  I know it&#8217;s satire, but maybe it&#8217;s &#8216;really&#8217; only 10% exaggerated, or perhaps the public perceive it as being very close to the truth. 

Perhaps viewers are too removed. Again it&#8217;s hard not to mention *Frontline*. It sent-up something we see every day; current affairs.  Viewers had a buy in and could relate to it. This Canberra public-service stuff may be too distant or not easy to relate to.

#### Suggestions

***The One with Phillip and Warren***. How about a show that turns things around. Spin off another thread.  We focus on Phillip and Warren, with Tony etc being bit parts. We see how P&W &#8216;really&#8217; work and how they relate to &#8211; and view &#8211; Tony et al.  They may be just as manipulative of Tony.

***The One where Things Stay Wrong.*** What happens when they can&#8217;t spin it. How does the gang cope? Do they turn on each other, find a scapegoat?

***The One with the Documentary Crew***.  The ABC is making a documentary on the Department. How does Tony explain their activities to a journalist who clearly knows what is really going on.  As she used to work there&#8230;
