Did this train trip on a weekday as that, from my reading, means it&#8217;s *off-peak* prices (assuming you do similar times to me). Pays to check. 

My main purpose was to revisit the excellent ***Ballarat Art Gallery***. It has a marvellous collection of Australian Art and is only about 5 minutes stroll from the main Ballarat train station. In fact, I don&#8217;t think I was ever more than about 15 minutes walk from the station during this whole trip.

This is a journey you can (must?) do using a Myki. You must top up well before you go, I&#8217;d suggest the day before. I paid full fare and it was **$26.88** Return. Here&#8217;s a quick summary of the fun day:
TimeItem8:43 am**Train** from North Williamstown to Footscray9:17 amV/Line Train leaves for Ballarat10:33 amTrain arrives at Ballarat (stops all stations)&#8211;Walk south down Lydiard St. Morning tea at one of the cafes&#8211;Visit **Art Gallery** in Lydiard St&#8211;Continue south towards Highway (Sturt St). **Lunch** here or nearby. A few nice places on South side of Sturt St (see Notes below)&#8211;Some post-lunch shopping and exploring of the area1:12 pmV/Line **Train** leaves Ballarat back to Melbourne2:17 pmV/Line Train arrives at Footscray (semi-express, picked on purpose)3:23 pmTrain leaves Footscray towards Williamstown3:33 pmTrain Arrives back at North Williamstown
Other notes:

- 
I had lunch at [DaVinci&#8217;s Italian Cafe](http://www.davincisballarat.com.au/) which is at 29 Sturt Street. I loved it. They had a &#8216;main and drink&#8217; lunch special

- 
The [Art Gallery of Ballarat](http://www.artgalleryofballarat.com.au/) web site has some useful information, including on their current exhibitions.

- 
I walked to the station from home. So this journey was almost exactly like someone would have done 100+ years ago.
