I’ve been looking at non-verbal communication tools for the iPhone. Most cost too much or generally were not fit for our purpose. So, I decided to create my own **free** Picture Board, but with two key criteria:

1. To use existing, free graphic/pictures/clip_art, respecting any licensing 
2. To use the in-built iPhone Photo viewer. It too is free with every iPhone/iPod Touch 

In summary, **it worked**! 

### Quick steps

I obtained the raw images, mainly from Open Clip Art, such as the *glass* one from&#160; [http://www.openclipart.org/detail/4431](http://www.openclipart.org/detail/4431)

[![flomar_Glass_(cup)](http://www.artwill.com.au/images/iPodiPhonePictureBoardidea_CE29/flomar_Glass_cup_thumb.png)](http://www.artwill.com.au/images/iPodiPhonePictureBoardidea_CE29/flomar_Glass_cup.png)

&#160;

I added my own text – and a white background if required.

[![drink](http://www.artwill.com.au/images/iPodiPhonePictureBoardidea_CE29/drink_thumb.jpg)](http://www.artwill.com.au/images/iPodiPhonePictureBoardidea_CE29/drink.jpg)

Created a new folder on the PC called MyPicBoard.&#160; Saved this updated graphic (above) as *drink.jpg* in this new folder. 

Repeated for the other images, like Bed, I Want, I See.

Synced the iPod Touch with my new MyPicBoard folder as the folder to be synced.

And look what appeared on the iPod Touch:

[![IMG_0020](http://www.artwill.com.au/images/iPodiPhonePictureBoardidea_CE29/IMG_0020_thumb.png)](http://www.artwill.com.au/images/iPodiPhonePictureBoardidea_CE29/IMG_0020.png)

All going well, when I touch MyPicBoard I should get a ‘dashboard’ of my Pictures. And yes indeed:

[![IMG_0021](http://www.artwill.com.au/images/iPodiPhonePictureBoardidea_CE29/IMG_0021_thumb.png)](http://www.artwill.com.au/images/iPodiPhonePictureBoardidea_CE29/IMG_0021.png)

If need be, I can touch one of them to see it in detail (or to confirm that’s what I want):

[![IMG_0022](http://www.artwill.com.au/images/iPodiPhonePictureBoardidea_CE29/IMG_0022_thumb.png)](http://www.artwill.com.au/images/iPodiPhonePictureBoardidea_CE29/IMG_0022.png)

I’m very happy with this, as a first effort. Some points of interest

1. Notice how the Verbs/Actions appear first (*I See, I Want*).&#160; The trick was that the iPod sorts the pictures by file name. So I called the files 1_iWant.jpg, 2_iSee.jpg ; these bubble to the top of the sort list. 
2. You could create multiple folders, for different actions, time of day, activities etc 
3. As I said, you can tailor it with your own images, phrases, have your own display order etc etc
