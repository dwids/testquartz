Not sure quite where these originated from or if they are copyright.

Some of the finest double entendres on British TV & Radio:

MICHAEL Buerk watching Phillipa Forrester cuddle up to a male

astronomer for warmth during BBC1&#8217;s UK eclipse coverage remarked,

&#8220;They seem cold out there, they&#8217;re rubbing each other and he&#8217;s only

come in his shorts.&#8221;

KEN Brown commentating on golfer Nick Faldo and his caddie Fanny

Sunneson lining-up shots at the Scottish Open: &#8220;Some weeks Nick likes

to use Fanny, other weeks he prefers to do it by himself.&#8221;

MIKE Hallett discussing missed snooker shots on Sky Sports: &#8220;Stephen

Hendry jumps on Steve Davis&#8217;s misses every chance he gets.&#8221;

JACK Burnicle was talking about Colin Edwards&#8217; tyre choice on World

Superbike racing: &#8220;Colin had a hard on in practice earlier, and I bet

he wished he had a hard on now.&#8221;

Chris Tarrant discussing the first Millionaire winner Judith Keppel

on This Morning: &#8220;She was practising fastest finger first by herself in bed

last night.&#8221;

WINNING Post&#8217;s Stewart Machin commentating on jockey Tony McCoy&#8217;s

formidable lead: &#8220;Tony has a quick look between his legs and likes

what he sees.&#8221;

ROSS King discussing relays with champion runner Phil Redmond: &#8220;Well

Phil, tell us about your amazing third leg.&#8221;

CRICKETER Neil Fairbrother hit a single during a Durham v Lancashire

match, inspiring Bobby Simpson to observe: &#8220;With his lovely soft

hands he just tossed it off.&#8221;

CLAIR Frisby talking about a jumbo hot dog on Look North said:

&#8220;There&#8217;s nothing like a big hot sausage inside you on a cold night

like this.&#8221;

JAMES Allen interviewing Ralf Schumacher at a Grand Prix, asked:

&#8220;What does it feel like being rammed up the backside by Barrichello?&#8221;

WILLIE Carson was telling Claire Balding how jockeys prepare for a

big race when he said: &#8220;They usually have four or five dreams a night

about coming from different positions.&#8221;

CARENZA Lewis about finding food in the Middle Ages on Time Team Live

said: &#8220;You&#8217;d eat beaver if you could get it.&#8221;

A FEMALE news anchor who, the day after it was supposed to have

snowed and didn&#8217;t, turned to the weatherman and asked, &#8220;So Bob,

where&#8217;s that eight inches you promised me last night?&#8221; Not only did

HE have to leave the set, but half the crew did too, because they

were laughing so hard!
