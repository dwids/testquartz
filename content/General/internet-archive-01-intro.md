This is one great, free resource. As they describe themselves, [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/index.php) is a &#8220;non-profit library of millions of free books, movies, software, music, websites, and more.&#8221; 

It has a number of &#8216;sections&#8217;, beside the base one (above).  The two I go to most recently are:

- The [Magazine Rack](https://archive.org/details/magazine_rack)
- The Books section of the Archive

## The Magazine Rack

As the name implies these are scanned-in old magazines. They are usually available to read online (in your browser) and download in assorted formats, like PDF or the strangely named Comic Book Zip. Don&#8217;t underestimate that last one; it&#8217;s usually a larger file than the PDF and better quality. They are usually just numbered jpg image files in a zip file.

There are some absolute gems at the Rack. Like this late 1975 edition of the Australian magazine [Cinema Papers,](https://archive.org/details/issuu_libuow_cinemapaper1975novno000) covering a new film called *Picnic at Hanging Rock*.

## Archived Books

The main Internet Archive has books. They fall into two broad categories:

- Ones you can view online and/or download, without logging in to the Archive or setting up extra (licensing) software. These are usually older books, like this 1888 (!) edition of [Goody Two-Shoes](https://archive.org/details/goodytwoshoes00newyiala/mode/2up) . That was just on the front page of the books section.
- Other books &#8211; more &#8216;modern&#8217; &#8211; require you to create an Archive account, log in and &#8216;borrow&#8217; them. These are more locked down, probably due to licensing requirements. You&#8217;ll also need extra software &#8211; for the licensing &#8211; and set up on your PC, but it&#8217;s not that difficult. You then download the book to your PC etc.  **I&#8217;ll write up some quick steps later.**

AFAIK, during this pandemic, the Archive has increased the number of users who can borrow the same book. Here&#8217;s the main page of the[ Books to Borrow](https://archive.org/details/inlibrary?sort=-publicdate) section.
