Back around 2000, Ian Kershaw wrote a large 2-volume biography of Hitler. Book 1 (*[Hubris](http://www.amazon.com/Hitler-1889-1936-Hubris-Ian-Kershaw/dp/0393320359/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&amp;s=books&amp;qid=1244960241&amp;sr=1-2)* as per above) is 912 pages.&#160; Found an ex-library, hardback version on the web for virtually nothing.&#160; Am 60 pages in already and yet Adolf is only 19 and WW1 hasn’t started yet. It’s a very well written text, with references everywhere.&#160; I ordered the second book *[Hitler: 1936-1945: Nemesis](http://www.amazon.com/Hitler-1936-1945-Nemesis-Ian-Kershaw/dp/0393322521/ref=pd_bxgy_b_text_b)* from [Better World Books](http://www.betterworldbooks.com/). Again a used hardback. It’s arrived and sitting on the To Read shelf. Well, when I say shelf, I mean pile.
