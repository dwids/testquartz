All of these were off-the-asphalt drives I&#8217;ve done over the years. Longish dirt roads and tracks.  At least two were before I had a 4WD!  

## Buchan to Corryong in a 1973 Cortina

About &#8217;83 or &#8217;84. I think this was the route. It was up and over the Snowy Mountains on unmade roads.  This route uses the Barry Way and the Great Alpine Way; either or both were not made then.  The car was manual, so I could do the old truckie&#8217;s &#8216;gear breaking&#8217; to slow down and try and stop the real brakes from overheating on the steep roads.

## Mildura to Lake Mungo (day trip) in a Ford Fairmont

This spontaneous one was probably the maddest &#8211; and yet most moving and memorable &#8211; drive I&#8217;ve ever done. The Fairmont was a company car.  From memory it was late 80s or early 90s.  I was staying at Mildura and had only vaguely heard of this Lake Mungo (that should really be &#8220;Lake&#8221; Mungo &#8211; it hasn&#8217;t held serious water for, umm, thousands of years)

I&#8217;d seen a sign pointing to it, so popped in to talk to a parks ranger about the trip/roads. This was in the afternoon. He said I could do in a 2WD, but to take it easy and watch out for kangaroos on the way back. He said the sunsets there are spectacular. No Google maps and I took a gulp when I saw the sign:  *118km.***Most of which was****unmade**&#8230;. (which he&#8217;d told me)

It took at least 1 and a half hours to get there. But I was gobsmacked at this stunning, beautiful place. I stayed to watch the sun setting and it was breathtaking.  I only found out later that human remains had been found here; dating back at least 40,000 years.

I then had to drive back. In the dark. On dusty, unmade roads. With kangaroos around.  90 minutes later, I arrived back &#8211; safely &#8211; at Mildura. Miraculously zero roo interactions but still buzzing with excitement. 

From that day onward, Lake Mungo has never let me go. I just love it and want to go back, for the 4th time.

## Menindee to Mildura (Nissan X-Trail)

Yes a 4WD, but it made me over-confident, which nearly brought me undone.

**Pause**…I had an idea and spent 90+ minutes looking for one (1) specific photo of this trip. First problem: I had no idea what year this trip was. I thought it was not long after I&#8217;d got the X-Trail. But what year was that and would they be digital shots or old photo/prints…most of which are not really catalogued. An old email implied it was 2003. Off I went to the boxes of Decade-2000s photos

And I&#8217;m back. I FOUND THE PHOTO!

Okay, so in Easter 2003, I&#8217;d driven the new X-Trail to Broken Hill (850km from Williamstown), then 110km over to Menindee to see it&#8217;s Lakes. That was great fun, but time to get home, via Mildura. 

The locals said I could drive south to Mildura, rather than back NW to Broken Hill then all the way back to Mildura. But it was **un-made roads**.

From memory about 150 km of them&#8230;
![](image-39.png)
All was going well. In fact too well. I was belting along on these isolated, dirt roads, hitting the cattle grids at 80 or so km/h. *How easy is this?* &#8211; I thought. 

Until the *thump, thump, thump* sound came from the rear passenger&#8217;s side. 

That tyre was flat to the rim. Caused by Mr Naïve-Overconfident hitting cattle grids at&#8230;you know the rest.

I put the one (1) spare on, then had to face about **100km of this unmade road with no spare.**

I was rather nervous and obviously driving very slowly over the remaining cattle grids.  I remember seeing asphalt at Pooncarie and knew I&#8217;m made it.  After stopping for a coffee, it was only another 125 km (*cough*) back to Mildura &#8211; on the made road &#8211; and the tyre place there.

Here&#8217;s the photo. Just after I&#8217;d changed the tyre.  
![](wheel-change-menindee-2002-scanned_20200428_205101.jpg)
I now remember the guide book had the *wrong *instructions; trying to get you to take the silver &#8216;mag wheel&#8217; bit off.  That just added to the slight 🙂 tension in the air.
