I appreciate that *The Age* – and other papers – are doing it tough.  **I am going to pay them for access to their content**. But the process and offerings are confusing.

They basically have 2 ‘digital’ offerings: Website Only or Website plus Tablet. I went for the Website plus Tablet. At $1 for the first month it, was a great deal (then only $25 pm after that)

## But what don’t I get?

My first issue is they tell me what I **do** have access to but – **relative to the printed paper** – it’s not obvious what I **don’t** have access to.  It may be buried in an FAQ (which I couldn&#8217;t find) or it may assume that somehow I know every section that the printed paper offers, so I can deduce. But it should be front and centre. There’s a line about crosswords being only iPad (I’m Android).

## Unlimited? 

Adding to the confusion they have included the ambiguous word *unlimited* in their descriptions – always a dangerous line to walk, particularly in the context of the previous issue.

## We value your questions….then ignore them

They aren’t responsive. I was directed to read more at [http://knownoboundaries.smh.com.au/blog/digital_subscriptions/](http://knownoboundaries.smh.com.au/blog/digital_subscriptions/)  Which I did. That page has a comments section, using which I asked similar questions (see at end of this blog post for copy) and I got acknowledgement that it was “awaiting moderation.”  This was 3 weeks ago – July 14th. And still nothing. Not published. Not answered.

## Can’t downgrade your subscription

Until I can clear things up, I decided to go back to the Website Only one for \$15 PM. Even then they don’t have a ‘downgrade’ option and their (late!) email said they had to cancel my current one and wait till the next billing period..and I had to manually re-sign for the \$15 one. Someone didn’t do their planning well. 

I certainly hope they improve this as I want them to succeed and I want to pay them.
