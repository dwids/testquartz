It&#8217;s cool to be wrong. Well perhaps that&#8217;s a bit harsh. Maybe not up to date actually.

I&#8217;d assumed (cough) that PC movie files  &#8211; specifically Xvid video stored in the AVI container file &#8211; could not have surround sound nor subtitles. Wrong, Jana, wrong.

I recently spotted a file with the long name of * filename.xvid.ac3.avi*   Mmm. AC3. That&#8217;s the correct name for what we call Dolby Digital.  So I tried this player on my [Neuston MC-500](http://www.hyperreality.com.au/hyper.php?section=products&amp;category=network_media_players) network media player. The amp&#8217;s *Dolby Digital* light fired up, shortly followed by all 6 speakers. Woo hoo. 5.1 surround sound!

Then I noticed that a foreign language show had a 2nd a file with it;* filename.xvid.ac3.sub*  Subtitles!  Double plus woo hoo.  Well not quite.  Subtitles are &#8216;attached&#8217; on-the-fly on the PC, but (sadly) not on the MC-500. Double plus ungood, my brothers. I think it&#8217;s time for my beloved [VirtualDub](http://www.virtualdub.org/) and a subtitle plugin. Should be able to create a copy of the avi with the subtitles permanently attached.  Darn that is one good program.
